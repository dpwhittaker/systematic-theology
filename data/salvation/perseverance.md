# Perseverance of the Saints: Can You Lose Salvation?

The question divides Christians. Can a genuine believer fall away and be lost?

**The stakes:**
- If you can't lose salvation, assurance is possible. You're secure in Christ.
- If you can lose salvation, assurance is fragile. You must persevere by your own effort.

**The dilemma:**
- Scripture warns against falling away (Hebrews 6:4-6).
- Scripture promises God will keep us to the end (Philippians 1:6).

**How do we reconcile both?**

## The Calvinist View: Perseverance of the Saints

**Also called "eternal security" or "once saved, always saved."**

**Summary:** True believers will persevere to the end because God preserves them. If someone appears to fall away permanently, they were never truly saved in the first place.

**Key principle:** Salvation is God's work from start to finish. If God [justified](#salvation/justification 'Greek') you, He will [glorify](#salvation/glorification 'Greek') you. The chain is unbreakable.

**Romans 8:30:** "Those whom he justified he also glorified."

Notice: Glorified is in the past tense (from God's eternal perspective). It's already certain.

**Philippians 1:6:** "I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ."

God finishes what He starts. He doesn't begin salvation and then fail to complete it.

**John 10:28-29:** "I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand. My Father, who has given them to me, is greater than all, and no one is able to snatch them out of the Father's hand."

**Jesus' promise:**
- Eternal life (not temporary life).
- They will *never* perish.
- No one can snatch them away (not Satan, not circumstances, not even themselves).

**The guarantee:** God's power, not human effort.

### Why Believers Persevere

**Not because they're strong, but because God holds them.**

**1 Peter 1:5:** "By God's power you are being guarded through faith for a salvation ready to be revealed in the last time."

**Jude 24-25:** "Now to him who is able to keep you from stumbling and to present you blameless before the presence of his glory with great joy..."

God is able. He keeps us. We don't keep ourselves.

**Romans 8:38-39:** "For I am sure that neither death nor life, nor angels nor rulers, nor things present nor things to come, nor powers, nor height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord."

Nothing can separate us—not even our own sin or weakness. God's love holds us.

### What About Those Who Fall Away?

**1 John 2:19:** "They went out from us, but they were not of us; for if they had been of us, they would have continued with us. But they went out, that it might become plain that they all are not of us."

**Interpretation:** Those who permanently abandon the faith were never genuinely saved. They appeared to be believers, but they lacked true saving faith.

**The evidence of genuine faith is endurance.**

**Matthew 24:13:** "The one who endures to the end will be saved."

Not: Endurance earns salvation.
But: Endurance proves salvation occurred.

**True faith perseveres.** If there's no perseverance, there was no true faith.

### Assurance: Based on Christ, Not Performance

**If perseverance is guaranteed, can I have assurance?**

**Yes.** Assurance is grounded in:
1. **God's promise:** He will complete what He began (Philippians 1:6).
2. **Christ's work:** Salvation depends on His finished work, not my continuing effort.
3. **The Spirit's witness:** "The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit that we are children of God" (Romans 8:16).

**The danger of basing assurance on performance:**

If I must persevere by my own strength, how do I know I'll make it? What if I fail tomorrow? Assurance becomes impossible.

**But if God preserves me, I can be confident.** Not in my strength, but in His.

**2 Timothy 1:12:** "I know whom I have believed, and I am convinced that he is able to guard until that day what has been entrusted to me."

Assurance is in God's ability, not mine.

## The Arminian View: Conditional Security

**Summary:** Believers can fall away and lose salvation if they abandon faith. Perseverance is necessary, but it's not guaranteed. God gives grace, but humans must cooperate.

**Key principle:** Salvation is God's work, but human free will remains. We can choose to reject God's grace, even after being saved.

**Hebrews 6:4-6:** "For it is impossible, in the case of those who have once been enlightened, who have tasted the heavenly gift, and have shared in the Holy Spirit, and have tasted the goodness of the word of God and the powers of the age to come, and then have fallen away, to restore them again to repentance."

**Interpretation:** These are genuine believers (enlightened, shared in the Spirit) who fell away. It's not that they were never saved; they lost their salvation.

**Hebrews 10:26-29:** "For if we go on sinning deliberately after receiving the knowledge of the truth, there no longer remains a sacrifice for sins, but a fearful expectation of judgment... How much worse punishment, do you think, will be deserved by the one who has trampled underfoot the Son of God, and has profaned the blood of the covenant by which he was sanctified?"

**Interpretation:** "Sanctified" indicates they were genuinely saved. But deliberate, persistent sin can result in losing that salvation.

**2 Peter 2:20-22:** "If, after they have escaped the defilements of the world through the knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ, they are again entangled in them and overcome, the last state has become worse for them than the first... The dog returns to its own vomit, and the sow, after washing herself, returns to wallow in the mire."

**Interpretation:** They "escaped" (were saved), but then returned to sin and lost their salvation.

### Why Perseverance Is Not Guaranteed

**Free will remains after salvation.** We can choose to reject God's grace.

**God doesn't force us to stay saved.** He enables, draws, sustains—but we must cooperate.

**John 15:6:** "If anyone does not abide in me he is thrown away like a branch and withers; and the branches are gathered, thrown into the fire, and burned."

Believers must *abide* in Christ. If they don't, they're cut off.

**Colossians 1:22-23:** "He has now reconciled [you]... if indeed you continue in the faith, stable and steadfast, not shifting from the hope of the gospel."

The "if" is conditional. Perseverance is required.

### Assurance: Hopeful but Not Certain

**Arminians emphasize:**

Assurance is possible, but it's based on present faith, not guaranteed future perseverance.

**1 John 5:13:** "I write these things to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life."

You can know you have eternal life *now*. But you must continue believing.

**Philippians 2:12:** "Work out your own salvation with fear and trembling."

There's a healthy fear—recognition that perseverance requires ongoing effort.

**The danger of false assurance:** Presuming "I'm saved, so I can live however I want" is dangerous. Faith without works is dead (James 2:17).

## Biblical Texts: Examining Both Sides

### Texts Supporting Perseverance (Calvinist)

**John 6:39:** "And this is the will of him who sent me, that I should lose nothing of all that he has given me, but raise it up on the last day."

Jesus will lose *none* whom the Father gave Him.

**Romans 8:28-30:** "For those whom he foreknew he also predestined... those whom he justified he also glorified."

The chain from predestination to glorification is unbroken.

**1 Corinthians 1:8-9:** "He will sustain you to the end, guiltless in the day of our Lord Jesus Christ. God is faithful, by whom you were called into the fellowship of his Son."

God sustains. He is faithful.

**Ephesians 1:13-14:** "In him you also, when you heard the word of truth, the gospel of your salvation, and believed in him, were sealed with the promised Holy Spirit, who is the guarantee of our inheritance until we acquire possession of it."

The Spirit is a *seal* and *guarantee*. Seals aren't easily broken.

### Texts Suggesting Apostasy Is Possible (Arminian)

**Matthew 24:10-13:** "And then many will fall away... But the one who endures to the end will be saved."

Falling away is a real danger. Endurance is necessary for final salvation.

**1 Corinthians 10:12:** "Let anyone who thinks that he stands take heed lest he fall."

Even those who "stand" (believers) can fall.

**Galatians 5:4:** "You are severed from Christ, you who would be justified by the law; you have fallen away from grace."

Paul warns believers they can be "severed from Christ" and "fall from grace."

**1 Timothy 4:1:** "Now the Spirit expressly says that in later times some will depart from the faith."

Departing from the faith implies they had the faith to begin with.

**Hebrews 3:12:** "Take care, brothers, lest there be in any of you an evil, unbelieving heart, leading you to fall away from the living God."

Warning to "brothers" (believers) not to fall away.

**Revelation 3:5:** "The one who conquers will be clothed thus in white garments, and I will never blot his name out of the book of life."

**Implication:** If names can be blotted out, salvation can be lost.

(Calvinist response: This is hypothetical. Names of the elect will never actually be blotted out.)

## How Each Side Interprets the Warning Passages

### Calvinist Interpretation of Warnings

**Warnings are real, but they're the means God uses to preserve His elect.**

**Hebrews 6:4-6** (enlightened, tasted the Spirit, then fell away):

**Calvinist response:**
1. This describes people who had close exposure to the gospel (tasted, enlightened) but never truly believed.
2. Or, it's a hypothetical warning: "If it were possible to fall away (which it's not for the elect), restoration would be impossible."

**The warnings serve a purpose:** They're how God ensures believers persevere. Warnings motivate vigilance.

**Analogy:** A parent warns a child, "Don't run into the street or you'll be hit by a car." The warning is real. The parent means it. But the parent also holds the child's hand, ensuring they don't run into the street. The warning and the protection work together.

Similarly, God warns believers ("persevere or you'll perish") while also preserving them so they do persevere.

**Philippians 2:12-13:** "Work out your own salvation with fear and trembling, for it is God who works in you, both to will and to work for his good pleasure."

We work because God works in us. The warning ("work out") and the guarantee ("God works in you") coexist.

### Arminian Interpretation of Assurance Texts

**Assurance texts describe God's intention and provision, not an unconditional guarantee.**

**John 10:28-29** (no one can snatch them from Jesus' hand):

**Arminian response:** No one *else* can snatch us—not Satan, not persecution. But we can choose to leave. The text doesn't say we can't walk away ourselves.

**Philippians 1:6** (God will complete the work):

**Arminian response:** God is faithful to complete the work *in those who remain in faith*. He doesn't force perseverance on those who reject Him.

**Romans 8:38-39** (nothing can separate us from God's love):

**Arminian response:** This speaks of external threats. God's love is constant. But we can reject that love.

**The analogy:** A husband may love his wife unconditionally, but she can still choose to leave the marriage. God's love doesn't fail, but humans can turn away.

## Middle Positions

### Moderate Calvinism

Some Calvinists soften "once saved, always saved":

- True believers will persevere, but assurance requires self-examination.
- If you're living in unrepentant sin, question whether your faith is genuine.
- Warnings are serious. Don't presume on grace.

**1 Corinthians 10:12:** "Let anyone who thinks that he stands take heed lest he fall."

### Moderate Arminianism

Some Arminians allow for strong assurance:

- You can have assurance while you're believing and obeying.
- Apostasy is possible, but if you're trusting Christ now, you're secure now.
- The focus is on present faith, not anxious fear about the future.

## Practical Implications

### For Assurance

**Calvinist strength:** Confidence in God's keeping power. Assurance based on Christ's finished work.

**Calvinist danger:** Presumption. "I prayed a prayer once, so I'm fine—no need to pursue holiness."

**Arminian strength:** Vigilance. Take warnings seriously. Don't presume.

**Arminian danger:** Anxiety. "Am I doing enough? Will I make it?"

### For Evangelism

**Calvinist:** Proclaim God's irrevocable promises. "If you believe, you're eternally secure."

**Arminian:** Call people to enduring faith. "Believe and continue believing."

### For Pastoral Care

**Calvinist approach to backsliders:** "If you're truly saved, God will bring you back. If you don't return, you were never saved."

**Arminian approach to backsliders:** "You're in danger of losing salvation. Return to Christ before it's too late."

## The Hebrew Framework: Covenant Faithfulness

In Hebrew thought, salvation is [covenant](#salvation/covenant 'Hebrew') relationship.

**God's side:** Unconditionally faithful. "I will never leave you or forsake you" (Hebrews 13:5).

**Human side:** Called to faithfulness. "Love the LORD your God with all your heart" (Deuteronomy 6:5).

**The question:** Can covenant relationship be broken by the human partner's unfaithfulness?

**Old Testament pattern:**

Israel repeatedly broke covenant (idolatry, disobedience). God exiled them (judgment). But He always restored them ([return](#salvation/return 'Hebrew')).

**Analogy to salvation:**

We're unfaithful (sin after conversion). Does God cut us off or restore us?

**Calvinist:** God keeps covenant no matter what. He brings us back.

**Arminian:** We can break covenant if we persistently rebel. God offers restoration, but we can refuse.

### Marriage Analogy

**Covenant marriage is "till death do us part."** Does that mean divorce is impossible?

**Legally, no.** People do divorce.

**But covenantally, yes.** Marriage is meant to be permanent.

**Similarly with salvation:**

**Calvinist:** True marriage (covenant union with Christ) cannot be dissolved. If it appears to dissolve, it was never real.

**Arminian:** Marriage can be broken by persistent unfaithfulness. God doesn't force us to stay.

## Eilu V'Eilu: Holding the Tension

Perhaps both sides are protecting essential truths:

**Calvinists protect:**
- God's sovereignty and faithfulness.
- The finished work of Christ (not dependent on us).
- Assurance grounded in God's promise.

**Arminians protect:**
- Human responsibility and free will.
- The seriousness of biblical warnings.
- The need for ongoing faith and obedience.

**Can both be true?**

**Perhaps the synthesis:**
- God's power guarantees that true believers will persevere.
- But we can't know with absolute certainty who the "true believers" are (including ourselves) until the end.
- So: Be confident in God's faithfulness, but also take warnings seriously.

**Hebrews 3:14:** "For we have come to share in Christ, if indeed we hold our original confidence firm to the end."

**Both confidence and vigilance.** Trust God's keeping power. And "work out your own salvation with fear and trembling."

The pilgrim's path: Secure in God's hand, yet pressing on toward the goal.
